Summary: 

- Typical equity split after 1st round in the Valley:

		VC							50%
		Options						30%
		Founders					20%
		Total						100%

- Honestly, the VC numbers are shockingly high to me. In China I've
    never seen any startup after 1st round with such an equity split
    that favors VCs so much.
    
- Typical equity split among founding team after 1st round in the Valley
on average:

		CEO							10%
		VP of Engineering			4%
		VP of Marketing				3%
		VP of Sale					1.5%
		Controller					1.0%
		1 ~ 5 Engineer				1.0% each
		5 ~ 10 Engineer				0.3% each
		Director of Marketing		0.5%
		Product Manager				0.2%

-   Michael asked a question about CFO. Lecturer's answer was straight:
    don't include CFO in your founding team, unless it's a hard-core
    financing business. Well, that's some encouraging news there.

- Do not recruit close friends into your startup, especially the best-man
on your wedding

- Vest the stocks for new members you invite to join the founding team. Do
not vest your own stocks (obviously).

- Do not give part-timers more than 1.0% stock. Do not partner with
someone that can only work part-time.

- Get the free interns from business schools. Make them hot.

- Use convertible bond (CB) for fund-raising. It's more effective and
efficient than the traditional equity fund-raising.

- Fill out Schedule C  (roles, responsibilities, deliverables, and
timeline) of LLC's legal document in details to minimize founder's
risks.

- Why some founders appear to be very generous with distributing their
stocks? - because when the company is small, they feel they have little
to lose, so they don't really care. When the company gets bigger and its
valuation gets higher, founder's mentality will change.
