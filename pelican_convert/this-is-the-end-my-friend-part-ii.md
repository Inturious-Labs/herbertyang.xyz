6.  My Way, Frank Sinatra

I wasn't too much into jazz music until I met Percy in a bar in Beijing.
Percy is a natural jazz singer and he filled my ears with Sinatra during
my five years in Beijing, as we hopped from bar to bar. Even without
smoking, I have a fairly thick and deep voice, so I picked up some
Sinatra songs as well as they are universally recognized and always
welcome on any party. This song is kinda showy and builds up emotions to
a nice climax. If I need one song to make an entrance at a party with
unknown audience, I'd go with this.

7.  Somebody, Depeche Mode

My CAS pal Xoua introduced me into the world of Depeche Mode and I heard
Somebody for the first time from another CAS pal, a Taiwanese girl, on a
bus during one of CAS' legendary D&D parties. In the days of CAS, we
said good-bye all the time, as we flew around the world not knowing when
we will see each other next. This beautiful and heartfelt song fit in
that time perfectly. I moved to Boston later and settled down there for
a few years, during which I tried to use this song on several occasions
(for example, when Sam left HPSC) but never quite found the perfect
moment. After spending a year making new friends from Sloan, I think
that moment has finally arrived.

8.  I Don't Want To Miss A Thing, Aerosmith

After Armageddon came out in 1998, I saw Steven Tyler performing this
song live on TV. I thought he was embarrassingly off-the-key most of the
time. Steven Tyler and Aerosmith slowly grow on me nonetheless over the
years and I gradually come to realize what a great rock vocalist he is,
especially after I founded my own rock band.Uma Thurman's endorsement of
"Dream On" on Be Cool (2005) certainly helped. I'm glad we have someone
like Sky in the Sloan class that can pull off the vocal feat of this
song.

9.  Wish You Were Here, Pink Floyd

When Yuxin left us to pursuit her career in theoretical mathematics, she
left a few CDs with me, including Duran Duran's Wedding Album and Pink
Floyd's Off The Wall. I was a late comer in Western music, but I was
lucky to have several friends who have unknowingly shared with me their
fine taste. Once given the rope, I climb up pretty quickly all by
myself. When living in Boston and Beijing, I started developing the
habit of never going on a road trip (greater than 2 hours) without
composing a music set list to enlighten myself and my passengers. My
music set list was usually an MP3 list from iPod, a self-burned CD, or
any album by Pink Floyd.

10. End of the Road, Boyz II Men

In the fall of 1994, I killed my days in Zhejiang University in
Hangzhou, China, by listening to all the Music Heaven CDs my roommate
had (probably 15-20 issues by then). That opened a door for me, and the
rest, is history.

This one was from one of the Music Heaven CDs. My [hobby of composing
music compilation
album](http://www.xiami.com/space/collect/u/1301213?spm=0.0.0.0.z0hTuJ) started
from Music Heaven. I'm very grateful to the team behind this
little-known music magazine (like a Chinese version of Spin magazine),
when rock and alternative music was still underground in China. Their
curatorship in music was amazing.
