Everybody says planning a beach party is easy - just go to a beach...
how hard can that be?

No. It is, actually, quite hard, when you have to plan a beach party for
60-80 people, with kids. There are many beaches in Northern
California/Bay area to choose from. The million-dollar question is,
which one is the best for the party?

Here's a list of some important attributes to keep in mind when you're
on the epic quest looking for that perfect beach.

-   **Distance**. Be aware of beach traffic. The weather is so excellent
    in California that everyone goes to beaches on weekends. When
    everyone is driving west toward beaches in Santa Cruz, the actual
    driving time could be 50% longer than suggested by Google Map.
     Little kids become restless in cars when driving time exceeds one
    hour.
-   **Water Temperature**. Water in certain beaches in California can be
    so cold (even in the summer) that no one could swim there. Beaches
    in the Santa Cruz area are in general warmer than beaches in the
    Half Moon Bay area.
-   **Parking**. This is the hardest thing. Parking on popular beaches
    (like the Main Beach in Santa Cruz) can be near impossible if you
    arrive there after noon. When you have a small party this is not a
    huge issue, but with a large group, you really have to be careful of
    the parking situation. Some beaches only have street parking nearby,
    with no parking lot. It's not uncommon to spend 20-30 minutes
    looking for parking for those late-comers.
-   **Terrain**. Some beaches are just not kids-friendly because they're
    too rugged and people have to climb down small cliffs to get to the
    beach. No good. Some beaches are very reclusive. The tranquility is
    good for small groups to chill out, but may not work for large
    groups because people get bored pretty quickly.
-   **Restaurants**. You might need to have two meals on a beach party.
    If restaurants are available in walking distance that would help a
    lot. With no restaurants, people have to either bring boxed food or
    have BBQ on the beach. Many wonderful, smaller beaches have no
    restaurants nearby.
-   **Bathroom/Bathroom**. Don't take it for granted. California state
    government is pretty broken on the budget side. For smaller beaches,
    make sure the bathroom is functioning before the trip. On our due
    diligence trip to Venice Beach in Half Moon Bay, we found the
    restroom was not functioning. Not cool.
-   **Lifeguard Tower**. Parents will have a lot more assurance and
    comfort if there is lifeguard on duty on the beach. Not every beach
    has that.
-   **BBQ**. People love having BBQs on the beach, but not every beach
    can provide that/allow that. More established beaches may have fixed
    BBQ grill stands. Some beaches allow for BBQ, but you have to carry
    your own BBQ Grill (those mobile ones).
-   **Dogs-allowed?** It's nice to have dogs running around, but many
    beaches do not allow that.
-   **Alcohol-allowed**? It appears that for most beaches, alcohol is
    not allowed (so people drink in bars along boardwalks like on Venice
    Beach in LA)
-   **Volleyball Court/Net.** Always a crowd-pleaser. Without
    volleyball, you can't make a party. It's easy to get volleyball, but
    it's not so easy to set up the net. Some large beaches have already
    set up beach volleyball nets.
-   **Wind**. When wind gets really strong, you can't do anything on the
    beach, including volleyball, and it's freezing. You could only find
    out about this through experience, or someone who's been there.
    Those highly commercialized large beaches usually have only little
    to moderate wind.
-   **Commercialization**. Fame doesn't always translate into fun.
    Highly commercialized beaches like Cowell Beach in Santa Cruz are
    filled with tourists. Those may not be your best option for a party.
-   **Bonfire**. Only a limited number of beaches allow bonfire. If you
    can get a bonfire going after sunset, your party will be a hit for
    sure. Those fire rings are sometimes on first-come, first-serve
    basis. In order to secure one, you might have to send someone to get
    to the beach really really early, like no later than 9am.
-   **Variety of Off-Beach Activities**. Roller-Coasters, rides, arcade
    games, bars, basketball courts, street performers... everything
    helps to prevent people from getting bored.
