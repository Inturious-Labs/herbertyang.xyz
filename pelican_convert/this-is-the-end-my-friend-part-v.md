21. Too Much Love Will Kill You, Queen

I wish I could play piano, so that I could perform this song. What a
great song to enthrall everyone in the room.

22. Don't Cry, Guns N' Roses

My cousin introduced me into Guns N' Roses' landmark album Use Your
Illusion when it first came about in bootlegged version in China. It
might have been 20 years since I saw him the last time. I went to Guns
N' Roses concert in Boston when living there between 2004 - 2007 (during
which I went to concerts of some of the biggest names in music history).
In bona fide GNR fashion, the band was late for three hours. Just as we
almost gave up waiting and the stadium was close to seeing a riot among
angry fans, lights went off and Axl Rose's Welcome-To-The-Jungle opening
scream pierced through the darkness and set the crowd on fire.

23. Kiss Me Goodbye, Tat Ming Pair (Hong Kong)

Tat Ming Pair (达明一派) represents the only entry on this list from
non-English speaking regions for two reasons: 1) I've been a huge fan of
them for the past 20+ years and considering them the best Chinese music
act of all-time. 2) I performed this song in my middle-school (that is,
before high-school). In my school (by the way, it was widely regarded as
having the best high-school in all China...I almost feel apologetic for
coming from such an elite school ... but well, on second thought, I
really don't), we used to have this 15-minute session of "Sing Everyday"
(天天唱) after lunch before afternoon class started. We usually used
this session to learn some pop songs from Hong Kong and Taiwan (there
was no pop music in Mainland China yet). I was 14-15 at that time. One
day this great idea stroke me like a lighting bolt: why don't I have a
singing tour to other classes to ... entertain them? So off I went and
did just that with a few classes in the same grade year. I sang 2-3
songs in every class during "Sing Everyday". There was no microphone, no
speakers, no music, just me standing behind teacher's stand to perform
solo, while students from other classes watching in awe, shock,
disbelief, or the mixture of all three. In retrospective, that act
sounds definitely silly and probably cute. If anything, it showed that I
had a natural inclination toward showmanship and spotlight at that age.
This song came from that set list.

24. We Will Rock You / We are the Champions, Queen

The swan song for the greatest rock n' roll band ever for Stanford GSB
Sloan Fellows, The Spillovers. The perfect song to close out an era. The
perfect song for the final curtain call. The perfect song to say goodbye
on a high note.

25. The End, The Doors

After all, this is supposed to be sad. After all, this is the end of
all. Hello, Jim Morrison.

-   The End -
