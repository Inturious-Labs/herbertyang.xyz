16. The Long and Winding Road, George Michael

Wham! was the first major western music act to grace the stage in China.
George Michael was so impossibly handsome at that time. Probably only
Pierce Brosnan and Takeshi Kaneshiro in their prime could rival the
good-looking of GM. Oh well, GM is also an incredible and very much
underrated singer (just like Prince is a highly underrated guitar
player). I don't think people gave him enough credit for his vocal
skills that he deserved in his Wham days. It took a [collaboration
between GM and the remains of Queen in the epic performance of "Somebody
To Love"](http://youtu.be/8c7x2JD_j-0)for the world to truly realize how
powerful GM's voice can be. Many fans consider GM's version is just as
good as, if not better, than Freddie Mercury's original. GM can also be
amazingly soulful and sentimental, like in this one.

17. Can You Feel the Love Tonight, Elton John

When I was in the first year of my undergraduate study in NUS in
Singapore, I performed this song during a Chinese MOE scholar party.
There was no background music, no speaker, just me soloing in front of
100-200 people. It went well until I forgot about the lyrics for maybe 5
seconds. Well, it was actually not as bad as it sounds. My voice
generally goes well with Elton John songs. I really need to learn piano
though.

18. Every Breath You Take, The Police

Sting looked dashing in this original MTV. Later our rock band The
Spillovers performed this song in Stanford Coho. We were lucky to have
Sky in our class, with such a good voice.

19. Your Song, Elton John

Another song to make a stir in any room, with only the first line "just
a little bit funny ...". So powerful, so much presence.
