MS&E 472 Entrepreneurial Thought Leader Seminar
("[ETL](http://etl.stanford.edu)")

The two co-founders of [Smule](http://www.smule.com) gave us an
interesting presentation. CEO [Jeff
Smith](http://ridingeast.blogspot.com) is a serial
entreprenur-turned-Stanford PhD student; CTO [Ge
Wang](https://ccrma.stanford.edu/~ge/) is an assistant professor of
Stanford.

Their company Smule has produced a variety of innovative iOS apps that
combine music with mobile technology, with clear source of revenue. One
of their flagship apps is a karaoke app called Sing!. It's gotta be the
grand-daddy of the massively popular Chinese karaoke iOS app - 唱吧,
which is not available to iTunes US users. Sing! falls into the same app
category as Instagram - they really should have been invented by me. I
have every reason to strike that idea first and bring it to the market
before others do, but I did not. Shame on me!

Something else stroke me during his presentation: all the
applications/softwares that exist today are meant to connect people at
the same time, ie. in the same physical universe/time dimension. Smule
is a classic demonstration of the kind of power and beauty such
connection can create. What if we try to connect people in different
time dimensions? For example, imagine Smule runs for 1,000 years, and
fuses voices/singing of people from different time era together. What
kind of effect will that create? Are people willing to pay for a dim
wish to talk to/sing with people in the future?

Before the session started, I was chatting to C on Professor Schramm's
Executive Communication class and the feedback he received. During
Jeff's presentation, Jeff mentioned that in the first 30 seconds of an
app's user experience, the app has to grab the user with excitement and
comfort. This sounds very similar to the crux of executive
communication, ain't it?

Question: what was your strategy? Answer: we had no strategy when
raising the first round. The investors took a gamble on us, even though
no one really believed in our business plan then (that was the time when
Nokia still ruled the world of mobile devices)

Question: how was your business model evolved? Answer: business model
changes, product changes, the team and the culture are the only things
that matter in the long-run.
