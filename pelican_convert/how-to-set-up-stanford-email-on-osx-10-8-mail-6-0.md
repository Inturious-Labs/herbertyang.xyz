Thanks to [Sten's](http://sten.tamkivi.com) help, I finally was able to
set up my Stanford email on the Mail client on my Macbook Air. For other
Sloans who might need similar help, here are the instructions:

Background: I used to set up my Gmail in a way such that all my other
emails will be routed through Gmail (I have 10+ from all sorts of
different projects/companies/organizations I'm involved in). Not only I
can receive those emails through my Gmail account, but also I can send
emails out in the name of my other email addresses (for example,
<herbert@wuzuoren.org>) through my Gmail. This is all pretty neat, until
recently Stanford IT made a slight change to its mail server setting
that spoiled my perfect world of emailing. Basically, I won't be able to
send email to restricted group mailing lists (for our Sloan Fellow
class) hiding behind my Gmail, like I used to. For an aspiring social
chair, spamming ability to the class on 24/7 basis is a must-have. So I
have to change the way I route my Gmail. Instead of consolidating
Stanford Email into my personal Gmail, I would have to route Stanford
Email to go to my email client on Macbook Air, Mail (version 6.0)
directly.

A few things to note:

- Incoming Mail Server is the trick here, as it should be
imap.gmail.com, not the XXX.pobox.stanford.edu from Stanford IT's
website.

- Use your gsb.stanford.edu email, not the stanford.edu one.
<XXX@stanford.edu> will expire after your graduation, but
<XXX@gsb.stanford.edu> is permanent.

- Turn off all the forwarding for your Stanford email to avoid
duplicated mails. In your Stanford Webmail, turn off forwarding to other
mail services like gmail; on your accounts.stanford.edu page, only send
mails to stanford.edu address.

Voila!
